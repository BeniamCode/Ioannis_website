Biography

Ioannis Dimitriadis’s (b. 1988) first steps as a pianist were guided by his curiosity at the age of five. When he was six years old he started taking piano lessons at the Modern Conservatory of Thessaloniki. From there he continued his musical education, studying under the great Greek conductor and pianist Georgios Thymis. In 2007 he completed his Theoretical education and acquired his degree of harmony. In 2008 he was admitted to the State Conservatory of Thessaloniki and the same year he received the "Distinguished Student Award" in the department of piano, by the same institution, for achieving the highest grades for the whole school year. In 2011 he graduated with a diploma in piano (Bachelor of Music), from the State Conservatory of Thessaloniki, where he studied under Prof. Patroklos Georgiadis. In 2012 he was admitted to the "Mannheim University of Music and Performing Arts" (Staatliche Hochschule für Musik und Darstellende Kunst Mannheim) in Germany, where he studied his Master of Music (Künstlerische Ausbildung) degree in performance under Prof. Michael Hauber, and he received the highest distinction for his final graduation recital in 2014.

He has taken part in many master classes and seminars conducted by distinguished pianists, such as Michael Hauber, Rudolf Meister (Germany), Martino Tirimo (England), Brandt Fredriksen, Goeffry Haydon (USA), Miki Itoh (Japan), Georgios Thymis, Pavlos Dimitriadis, Dimitris Toufeksis, Giorgos Kuratsos (Greece), etc.

He has performed numerous times as a soloist in concerts in Greece such as: the Auditorium Main Hall of the Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, and in many other stages and halls of his hometown Thessaloniki,in Athytos,etc. He has appeared in solo piano recitals as a special guest in “Music Mondays” events which were organized by the Conservatory of Northern Greece, in Byzitsa Pilio conducted by Unlimited Music and Arts , as well as performing solo recitals in Ludwigshafen and Mannheim in Germany. He has also collaborated with multifarious chamber music ensembles with many appearances in Europe.
